Information about Breast Cancer:

About Breast Cancer

Breast cancer is a type of cancer that starts in the breast tissue. It can occur in both men and women, but it is far more common in women. Early detection significantly improves outcomes. Our system provides two ways to predict breast cancer:

Symptoms
  • New lump in the breast or underarm (armpit)
  • Thickening or swelling of part of the breast
  • Irritation or dimpling of breast skin
  • Redness or flaky skin in the nipple area or the breast
  • Pulling in of the nipple or pain in the nipple area

Breast Cancer Prediction Models:

1. Image-Based Ultrasound Model

This model uses breast ultrasound images to detect:

  • Benign conditions
  • Malignant conditions
  • Normal (healthy) tissue

Accuracy: 85.70%

2. Tabular Data Model

This model predicts breast cancer risk using patient clinical data such as:

  • Age of patient
  • Tumor size and type
  • Other clinical risk factors

Accuracy: 93.66%
